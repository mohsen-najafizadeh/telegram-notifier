# Code of Conduct

We are committed to creating a welcoming and inclusive environment for all contributors, regardless of their background, experience, or expertise. By participating in this project, you agree to abide by the following code of conduct.

## Our Pledge

We pledge to make participation in this project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contribute to creating a positive environment include:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Showing empathy towards other community members
- Supporting a diverse and inclusive environment

Examples of unacceptable behavior by participants include:

- The use of sexualized language or imagery and unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or electronic address, without explicit permission
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported by contacting the project team at [your-email@example.com]. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. The project team is obligated to maintain confidentiality with regard to the reporter of an incident.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all spaces managed by the project, including GitHub repositories, discussion boards, and any other platform where the project is represented.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Project maintainers will follow these guidelines to resolve conflicts:

1. **Correction**: A maintainer may ask the participant to cease the inappropriate behavior.
2. **Warning**: If the behavior continues, the maintainer may issue a formal warning.
3. **Temporary Ban**: If the behavior persists, the maintainer may temporarily ban the participant from the community.
4. **Permanent Ban**: In cases of severe misconduct, a permanent ban from the project may be issued.
